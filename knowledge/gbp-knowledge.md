# Google Business Profile (GBP) Knowledge Base

> Full management guide for Google Business Profile — setup, optimization, ongoing management.
> Applies to US-based local businesses.

---

## Why GBP Matters

- GBP is the #1 local SEO ranking factor for "near me" and city-based searches
- Verified GBP = instant entity recognition by Google
- Map Pack (top 3 local results) drives 44%+ of local clicks
- GBP is free — highest ROI tool in local SEO
- Directly feeds Google's Knowledge Panel for the business

## GBP Setup Checklist (New Profile)

### Step 1 — Create or Claim
- Go to business.google.com
- Search for the business first — don't create a duplicate
- If already exists → click "Claim this business"
- If not → click "Add your business"

### Step 2 — Basic Info (Must Be Exact)
- **Business name** — exactly as it appears on signage and website (no keyword stuffing)
- **Category** — Primary category is the most important ranking factor (choose the most specific one)
- **Address** — full street address (must match NAP everywhere)
- **Service area** — if no storefront, set service area radius instead of hiding address
- **Phone number** — local area code preferred over 800 numbers
- **Website** — link to homepage (or specific landing page if multi-location)
- **Hours** — all days, include holiday hours

### Step 3 — Verification
- Options: postcard (5-7 days), phone, email, instant (if eligible), video
- Most businesses require postcard — mail goes to the business address
- After verification code arrives: enter it in GBP dashboard
- Cannot fully optimize until verified

### Step 4 — Complete All Sections
After verification, fill in:
- [ ] Description (750 characters — use first 250 for key info)
- [ ] Services (list all services with descriptions)
- [ ] Products (if applicable)
- [ ] Attributes (women-led, veteran-owned, wheelchair accessible, etc.)
- [ ] Opening date
- [ ] Logo (square, high quality)
- [ ] Cover photo (landscape, represents the business)
- [ ] 10+ additional photos
- [ ] Q&A section (seed with your own questions and answers)

## GBP Optimization (After Setup)

### Business Name
- Use EXACT legal/brand name — no keyword stuffing
- Bad: "Joe's Plumbing | Best Plumber Houston TX"
- Good: "Joe's Plumbing"
- Keyword stuffing in name = suspension risk

### Primary Category
- Most important ranking factor in GBP
- Be as specific as possible
- Example: "Plumber" instead of "Contractor"
- Example: "Italian Restaurant" instead of "Restaurant"
- Research: search competitors in Maps and see their categories

### Secondary Categories
- Add up to 9 additional categories
- Cover all services you offer
- Example for a dentist: Dentist (primary) + Cosmetic Dentist + Dental Clinic + Emergency Dental Service

### Business Description
- 750 characters max (first 250 shown without clicking "more")
- Lead with your strongest value proposition
- Include primary keyword naturally
- Do NOT include links, phone numbers, or promotional language
- Formula: [Business name] is a [type] in [city] serving [who]. We specialize in [services]. [Why choose us] [CTA].

### Services Section
- Add ALL services with names, descriptions, and prices (if applicable)
- Service names = keywords — use what people search for
- Descriptions: 300 characters max, be specific
- This directly influences what searches you appear for

### Photos — What to Upload
| Photo Type | Details |
|---|---|
| Logo | Square, 720x720px minimum, transparent or white bg |
| Cover | 1024x576px landscape, shows the business |
| Interior | Show the space — customers want to see where they're going |
| Exterior | Street view, signage visible — helps customers find you |
| Team/staff | Real photos of the team build trust |
| Products/work | Show what you do — before/after, finished work |
| Menu (if food) | Upload all menu pages |

- Minimum 10 photos to start
- Add new photos every 1-2 weeks
- Photos with people get 35% more engagement
- Never use stock photos

### Q&A Section
- Seed 5-10 common questions and answer them yourself
- Questions people actually ask before hiring/visiting
- Include keywords naturally in answers
- Monitor weekly — anyone can post questions, you must answer fast
- Flag spam or incorrect Q&As

## GBP Posts (Weekly)

Posts expire after 7 days (except Events/Offers). Post at least weekly.

### Post Types
| Type | Best For | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| Update | News, announcements, general content | 7 days |
| Offer | Promotions, discounts | Custom dates |
| Event | Webinars, open houses, special events | Event dates |
| Product | Showcase specific products/services | Until removed |

### Post Best Practices
- Photo required — use bright, relevant images (1200x900px)
- 150-300 words optimal (can go up to 1500)
- Include keyword naturally in first line
- Always add CTA button: Book, Call, Learn More, Visit, Order
- Post consistently — 1x/week minimum, 3x/week ideal
- Content ideas: tips, FAQs, case studies, promotions, before/after, team spotlights, seasonal content

### Post Ideas for Any Business
- "5 signs you need [service]"
- "How we helped a [customer type] with [problem]"
- "We're now offering [new service]"
- "[Season] special — [offer]"
- "Did you know? [interesting fact about your industry]"
- "[Team member] spotlight"
- "Check out our latest project"
- "[City] specific tips: [relevant local advice]"

## Review Management

Reviews are the #2 local ranking factor and #1 conversion factor.

### Getting More Reviews
- Ask every happy customer — timing matters (ask right after a positive interaction)
- Send a direct review link: go to GBP → Share profile → Copy link
- Add review link to email signature, invoice, follow-up texts
- Create a QR code linking to review page (for physical businesses)
- Never pay for or fake reviews — instant suspension
- Never ask for reviews in bulk at one time — looks suspicious

### Responding to Reviews (Every Single One)

**Positive review response formula:**
- Thank them by name
- Mention the specific service/product
- Add a keyword naturally
- Invite them back

Example:
> "Thank you so much, Sarah! We're glad the roof repair went smoothly and that your home is protected before the winter. It was great working with you. Hope to see you again if you ever need us!"

**Negative review response formula:**
- Acknowledge the issue (don't argue)
- Apologize (even if it wasn't your fault)
- Offer to resolve offline (give phone/email)
- Keep it short — don't over-explain

Example:
> "Hi John, we're sorry to hear your experience didn't meet expectations. This isn't the standard we hold ourselves to. Please reach out to us directly at hello@company.com so we can make this right."

**Never:**
- Argue with a reviewer
- Copy-paste the same response
- Ignore negative reviews
- Ask Google to remove a review unless it violates policy

### Flagging Fake or Spam Reviews
- In GBP dashboard → Reviews → Flag as inappropriate
- Give Google 3-5 business days to review
- If urgent or high impact, escalate via Google Business Profile Support

## GBP Insights — What to Monitor Monthly

| Metric | What It Tells You |
|---|---|
| Searches (Direct) | People searching your brand name |
| Searches (Discovery) | People finding you via category/keyword search |
| Views on Search | How many saw your profile in search results |
| Views on Maps | How many saw you in Google Maps |
| Direction requests | Intent to visit |
| Phone calls | Direct leads from GBP |
| Website clicks | Traffic sent to your site |
| Photo views | Engagement with your images |

**Key insight:** If Discovery searches are low → you're not ranking for category keywords → optimize primary category + add more services.

## GBP Suspension — How to Avoid It

Common reasons for suspension:
- Keyword stuffing in business name
- Fake address or PO Box
- Using a virtual office address without physical presence
- Creating duplicate listings
- Sudden large spike in reviews
- Mismatched info between GBP and website

If suspended:
1. Fix the issue that caused it
2. Submit reinstatement request: support.google.com/business
3. Include proof of business (photos, utility bills, licenses)

## Multi-Location GBP Strategy

- Each location = separate GBP listing
- Each location must have unique phone number and address
- Do NOT use same website URL for all — each should link to a location-specific page
- Consistency: same brand name format, same category structure

---

## GBP + Website Connection

- GBP and website must tell the same story
- NAP on website footer must EXACTLY match GBP
- Create a location page on the website with:
  - Full address
  - Embedded Google Map
  - Business hours
  - Local phone number
  - City-specific content (mention the area you serve)
  - Schema markup: LocalBusiness

## GBP Ranking Factors (Ranked by Impact)

1. Primary category (highest impact)
2. Keywords in business name (risky — don't keyword stuff)
3. Proximity to searcher
4. Review quantity and rating
5. Review recency (fresh reviews matter)
6. Review responses
7. GBP completeness (all fields filled)
8. Photo count and recency
9. Post frequency
10. Website authority and relevance
11. Citation consistency (NAP)
12. Engagement (clicks, calls, direction requests)
