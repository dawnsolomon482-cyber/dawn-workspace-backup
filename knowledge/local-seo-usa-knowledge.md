# Local SEO — USA Knowledge Base

> US-specific local SEO strategies, directories, citation sources, and ranking tactics.
> Client context: US-based local business targeting customers in their city/region.

## How Local SEO Works in the USA

Google shows 3 types of results for local searches:
1. **Map Pack** — top 3 GBP listings (most clicks, most valuable)
2. **Organic results** — standard website rankings below the map
3. **AI Overviews** — AI-generated summaries citing trusted sources

The goal: rank in the Map Pack AND organic results for the same keywords.

**Map Pack ranking factors (US):**
- GBP relevance (category, services, description)
- Proximity to searcher
- Prominence (reviews, citations, backlinks, website authority)

## US Keyword Research for Local SEO

### Local Keyword Formats
- `[service] [city]` — "plumber Houston"
- `[service] near me` — "plumber near me"
- `best [service] in [city]` — "best dentist in Austin"
- `[service] [neighborhood]` — "coffee shop downtown Denver"
- `[service] [city] [qualifier]` — "emergency plumber Dallas TX"
- `[adjective] [service] [city]` — "affordable divorce lawyer Chicago"

### Keyword Intent Tiers
| Tier | Type | Example | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|
| BOFU | Ready to hire | "emergency AC repair Dallas" | Highest |
| MOFU | Comparing options | "best HVAC company Houston" | High |
| TOFU | Researching | "how much does HVAC cost" | Medium |

### How to Find Local Keywords
1. Google autocomplete — type "[service] [city]" and note suggestions
2. "People Also Ask" on Google — questions to turn into content
3. "Related searches" at bottom of Google results page
4. Ahrefs / SEMrush — filter by location, sort by low KD
5. Google Search Console — see what you already rank for, find gaps
6. Competitor GBP — look at their services and categories

## Citation Building Process for US Clients

### Phase 1 — Core Citations (Week 1-2)
1. Google Business Profile — create/claim, verify, complete
2. Apple Maps — claim via mapsconnect.apple.com
3. Bing Places — connect to GBP for sync OR manual entry
4. Yelp — claim and complete profile
5. Facebook Business Page — complete all fields
6. LinkedIn Company Page
7. BBB listing — claim or create
8. Yellow Pages (yp.com)
9. Foursquare business listing

### Phase 2 — Authority Citations (Week 2-3)
10. Chamber of Commerce (local chapter + national directory)
11. Manta
12. Hotfrog
13. Nextdoor business profile
14. Industry-specific directories (pick top 5 for their niche)

### Phase 3 — Supplemental (Week 3-4)
15. Alignable
16. Bark.com
17. Thumbtack (for service businesses)
18. Additional industry directories

**Total target:** 25-40 consistent citations for a competitive US market.

### Citation Consistency Rules
- Business name: EXACTLY the same everywhere (Joe's Plumbing, not Joe's Plumbing LLC vs Joe's Plumbing)
- Address: same abbreviations (St vs Street, Ave vs Avenue — pick one)
- Phone: same format everywhere (555-123-4567 or (555) 123-4567 — pick one)
- Website URL: always use the same version (with www or without, with or without trailing slash)

## US Local SEO — On-Site Requirements

### Location Page (Essential for Local Rankings)
Every local business needs a location/service area page on their website:
- H1: "[Service] in [City, State]"
- Full NAP (Name, Address, Phone) — must match GBP exactly
- Embedded Google Map (with the GBP location pinned)
- Business hours
- Service list with descriptions
- Local landmark/neighborhood references ("serving [city] and surrounding areas including [suburb1], [suburb2]")
- Customer testimonials from local customers
- Local photos (not stock)
- LocalBusiness schema with address

### If Serving Multiple Cities
- Create a separate page per city (no thin content — each must be unique 500+ words)
- Do NOT create pages for cities you don't actually serve
- Each page: "[Service] in [City, State]" targeting different keywords
- Link all location pages from the main navigation or a "Service Areas" page

### Blog Content for US Local SEO
- "[City] guide to [topic related to your service]"
- "How much does [service] cost in [City]?" (high commercial intent)
- "Best [service] in [City, State]: What to look for"
- "[Service] laws/regulations in [State]" (evergreen, authoritative)
- "[Season] [service] tips for [City] homeowners"
- "We served [City neighborhood] — here's what happened" (case studies)

## US Review Strategy

### Best Review Platforms for US Local SEO
1. Google (GBP) — most important for rankings
2. Yelp — most consumer-trusted for certain niches
3. BBB — high trust signal, especially older demographics
4. Industry-specific (Houzz for contractors, Healthgrades for medical, Avvo for legal)
5. Facebook — social proof

### Review Velocity
- Google wants consistent reviews over time — not 50 at once then nothing
- Aim for 1-4 new Google reviews per month minimum
- Spread review requests across platforms (not all Google)

### Review Request Best Practices (US)
- Text message has 98% open rate — best channel
- Email works for B2B
- "Would you mind leaving us a quick Google review?" — direct ask works
- Send the direct link (no friction)
- FTC rules: never pay for reviews, never create fake reviews
- HIPAA note: if medical client — never respond to reviews in a way that confirms the patient relationship

## US Competitor Analysis for Local SEO

### How to Research Local Competitors
1. Search "[service] [city]" in Google — note the Map Pack top 3
2. Click their GBP — check: review count, rating, categories, posts, photos
3. Check their website — note content quality, location pages, schema
4. Run their domain in Ahrefs — see their backlinks and ranking keywords
5. Find citation gaps — where they're listed that your client isn't

### Beating a Competitor in the Map Pack
- More reviews + higher rating → request reviews consistently
- More relevant categories → add all applicable secondary categories
- More posts → post 2-3x per week vs their 1x
- More photos → upload weekly
- More complete profile → fill every field they're missing
- Better website authority → build backlinks from local sources (newspapers, chambers, local blogs)

## US Local Link Building

Links from local and relevant sources boost both organic rankings and GBP prominence.

### Local Link Sources
- **Local newspaper / news sites** — get mentioned in a local story
- **Chamber of Commerce** — join and get a member directory link
- **Local business associations** — join relevant trade groups
- **Sponsor local events** — most events list sponsors with links
- **Local blogs** — guest post or get featured
- **Partner businesses** — exchange links with complementary non-competitors
- **University or school sites** — if you offer scholarships, internships, or donate
- **Local .gov sites** — if you're a vendor or contractor for government
- **HARO / Connectively** — respond to journalist queries (get featured in national press)

## US State-Specific Considerations

- Some states have strict regulations on certain industries (legal, medical, financial)
- Content claims must be compliant with state law (especially health claims)
- Finance/legal businesses may need disclaimers
- HIPAA applies to medical businesses — review responses must not confirm patient relationship
- Check if client needs any specific licensing mentioned on site (builds trust + E-E-A-T)
